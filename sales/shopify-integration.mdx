The Shopify integration lets TokenSupply listen for paid orders and instantly deliver a digital key to your customer — no manual steps required. Once connected, fulfilment happens automatically from the moment a Shopify order is marked paid.

## Connecting Shopify

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Shopify integration page">
    Go to **Integrations → Shopify** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your credentials">
    Enter your **Shop Domain** (e.g. `your-store.myshopify.com`) and your **Admin API access token** (found under Shopify Admin → **Settings → Apps → Develop apps**).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Validate the connection">
    TokenSupply validates your credentials, lists your Shopify products, and shows the connection status. Use **Test Connection** on the integration page to confirm your credentials are working at any time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Mapping products

On any product's **Sales Channels** tab, map it to a Shopify variant. Each mapping has two settings:

- **Status** — `Active` means the mapping is live; `Paused` suspends it without deleting it.
- **Auto Deliver** — when enabled, TokenSupply automatically reserves and delivers a key the moment a matching Shopify order is paid.

You can also manage all Shopify mappings in bulk from **Sales Channel → Shopify**, without opening each product individually.

<Warning>
  Only enable **Auto Deliver** on products that have sufficient stock. If inventory runs out, TokenSupply cannot fulfil the order and a backorder situation may occur.
</Warning>

## Order flow

When a paid Shopify order arrives, TokenSupply follows these steps automatically:

1. Reserves a key from your inventory
2. Fulfils the Shopify line item via the API
3. Emails the customer a tokenised delivery link to your customer portal

The customer clicks the link, reveals their key, and the order is marked completed. No manual action is required from you.

## Tips

<Tip>
  Keep your Admin API token scoped to the minimum permissions required. TokenSupply only needs read access to products and orders, and write access to fulfilment.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Use **Auto Deliver** only for products with healthy, ongoing stock. For limited-edition or low-stock products, consider leaving Auto Deliver off and fulfilling manually to avoid backorders.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  If a Shopify order is cancelled upstream, any key that was reserved is automatically released back to your inventory — no manual intervention needed.
</Tip>
