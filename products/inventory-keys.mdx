TokenSupply stores your digital keys in an encrypted vault — no key value is ever exposed by default. Every action taken on a key, whether adding it, revealing it, copying it, selling it, or replacing it, is logged with the actor's identity and a timestamp. This audit trail means you always know who accessed what and when.

## Adding inventory

Open a product's **Inventory** tab to add keys. Three methods are available:

### Single key

Click **Add Item** and enter one key inline. You can optionally set:

- **Expiry date** — useful for time-limited codes or gift cards
- **Notes** — internal context visible to your team

Single-key entry is best for ad-hoc additions or corrections.

### Bulk upload

Click **Add Item → Bulk** and paste your keys directly into the text area, one key per row, or upload a CSV or TXT file. The UI validates the input in real time before submission.

<Note>
  For **Account**-type products, use the format `username | password` per line when uploading in bulk.
</Note>

### From a purchase order

When a vendor submits a stock file on a confirmed purchase order, those keys flow directly into the product's inventory once the stock file is approved. You do not need to re-enter them manually — they appear under the **Inventory** tab with the PO number linked for traceability.

## Duplicate prevention

Every key is stored with a unique reference. If you attempt to add a key that already exists in your inventory, TokenSupply rejects it. In the bulk upload UI, duplicates are detected in real time and flagged inline before you submit — so you catch them before they hit your catalog, not after.

## Masked keys and Reveal

All key values are masked by default in every list and table view. To see the full value, click **Reveal** on any key row. This opens the **Key Reveal Modal**, which displays the unmasked value and a **Copy Key** button.

<Warning>
  There is no inline copy-to-clipboard on masked keys. Every reveal goes through the Reveal modal so the action can be logged. This is intentional — it ensures every key access is auditable, not just the ones someone remembered to log manually.
</Warning>

## Key statuses

Each key moves through a defined lifecycle. The current status appears as a badge on every row.

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Available** | Ready for sale or delivery — counts toward active inventory |
| **Reserved** | Held for a pending order — temporarily unavailable |
| **Sold** | Delivered to a customer — counts toward fulfilled inventory |
| **Invalid** | Marked non-functional — excluded from sale |

You can manually update a key's status from its row menu, or use bulk mode to update multiple keys at once.

## Replacements

When a key has been sold or marked invalid and needs to be swapped out, use **Replace Key** on the item. This lets you enter a new key value in place of the original. The replacement is recorded as a discrete event: the old value, the new value, the timestamp, and the actor are all preserved in the item's replacement history.

Replacement history is viewable per key by opening the history dialog from the item's row menu. If the replacement was triggered by a support ticket, the ticket reference is linked directly in the history entry.

## Activity log

Every action taken on a key is captured in a per-item activity log:

- **Add** — when the key was created and by whom
- **Reveal** — every time the value was unmasked, with actor and timestamp
- **Copy** — every clipboard copy from the Reveal modal
- **Status change** — transitions between Available, Reserved, Sold, and Invalid
- **Replace** — replacement events with old and new values
- **Ticket link** — when the key is linked to or unlinked from a support ticket

Open the activity log for any key by clicking the **Activity** icon on its row. You can also export your full inventory to CSV or TXT from the toolbar for offline auditing.
