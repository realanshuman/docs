Products are the central entity in TokenSupply — everything else in the platform attaches to them. Your inventory keys live under a product's **Inventory** tab, vendor pricing is tracked as offers against a product, sales channel listings map to a product, and every purchase order references one. Before you can stock inventory or connect a marketplace listing, you need a product in your catalog.

## Creating a product

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Add Product form">
    Go to **Products → Add Product** in the sidebar. The form uses a centred, step-by-step layout.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in product details">
    Complete the required fields:

    - **Product Name** — the display name across the platform
    - **SKU** — your internal stock-keeping unit identifier
    - **Brand** — the publisher or brand (e.g. Microsoft, Steam, Google)
    - **Functional Category** — pick from the strict catalog: Gaming, Software & Apps, Gift Cards, Subscriptions, Entertainment, Education, Security & VPN, Cloud Services, Operating Systems, or Productivity
    - **Product Type** — choose one of **Key**, **Account**, or **Gift Card**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set delivery configuration">
    Under **Listing Configuration**, select the expected delivery time:

    - **Instant** — delivered immediately on order
    - **Within 1 hour** — fulfilled within the hour
    - **Within 24 hours** — fulfilled by the next business day
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the product">
    Submit the form to create the product. It appears in your catalog immediately with the status you set.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Product statuses

Every product has one of three statuses that controls its visibility and sellability across the platform.

| Status | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| **Enabled** | Live and sellable — appears in listings and is eligible for order fulfillment |
| **Disabled** | Temporarily hidden — removed from active listings but all data is preserved |
| **Discontinued** | Archived — no longer sold; kept for historical order and reporting purposes |

You can change a product's status at any time from the product detail page using **More Actions**.

## Product detail page

Opening any product takes you to its detail page, which gives you a quick-stats strip (in-stock count, average cost, vendor count, open tickets) and five tabs:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Overview" icon="info">
    Core product fields — SKU, Brand, Category, Type, Status — plus a Summary card showing total vendor count, purchase orders, and inventory value.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Inventory" icon="package">
    All inventory keys for this product. Add, filter, bulk-manage, and reveal keys directly from this tab.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sales Channels" icon="globe">
    Active marketplace listings mapped to this product across your connected channels.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Vendor Offers" icon="tag">
    Per-vendor pricing and delivery times. Used to auto-fill purchase orders and compare sourcing costs.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Activity" icon="clock">
    A timestamped log of all significant actions taken on this product.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## List view

The **Products** list is a high-density view showing every product's status, available inventory, category, vendor count, and **Lowest Price** — the cheapest active vendor offer on record.

You can filter by status tab (All / Active / Draft / Archived), narrow by Category or Product Type, and sort by name, inventory, price, vendors, or date created.

<Tip>
  Use the **Lowest Price** column to spot pricing outliers across your vendor offers at a glance. If one vendor is significantly cheaper than the others, it's a signal to consolidate purchasing — or to check whether the offer is still active.
</Tip>

## Editing and managing

To modify a product, open it and select **More Actions → Edit Product**. All fields set at creation can be updated.

To change a product's visibility, use **More Actions** to switch between **Enabled**, **Disabled**, and **Discontinued** statuses. This is non-destructive — inventory, vendor offers, and historical orders are retained regardless of status.

To permanently remove a product, choose **More Actions → Delete Product**. You must type `DELETE` to confirm. Deletion unlinks all associated purchase orders and vendor mappings and cannot be undone.
