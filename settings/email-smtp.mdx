TokenSupply sends transactional emails for order deliveries, vendor invites, PO confirmations, and invoices. Configuring SMTP lets these emails come from your own domain and sender name, giving customers a consistent, branded experience.

## Configuring SMTP

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open email settings">
    Go to **Settings → Email Setup** and toggle the **Email Service** switch to **Active**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter SMTP credentials">
    Fill in all required fields:
    - **SMTP Host** — your mail server hostname (e.g. `smtp.gmail.com`)
    - **Port** — typically `587` for TLS or `465` for SSL
    - **Username** — your SMTP login (usually your email address)
    - **Password** — your SMTP password or app-specific password
    - **From Email** — the address that appears in the `From` field
    - **From Name** — the display name customers see (e.g. your store name)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose encryption">
    Select **TLS**, **SSL**, or **None**. TLS on port 587 is recommended for most providers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send a test email">
    Click **Save Settings**, then click **Send Test Email**. A test message is sent to your account email — confirm delivery before going live.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Fallback behaviour

If SMTP is not configured or a send attempt fails, TokenSupply automatically falls back to Resend for critical transactional emails — so customers and vendors are never blocked from receiving order deliveries, invites, or notifications.

## Emails sent by TokenSupply

| Email | Triggered by |
|-------|-------------|
| Order delivery | New order completion or re-send delivery action |
| Vendor invite | Invite to Portal on the vendor detail page |
| PO confirmation | Confirming a purchase order |
| PO status update | PO lifecycle transitions (Sent → Paid → Closed, etc.) |
| Invoice | Sending an invoice to a customer |
| Password reset | Reset password request from the sign-in page |

## Tips

<Tip>
If you use Gmail or Google Workspace, generate an **app-specific password** in your Google account security settings. Your main account password will not work when 2-Step Verification is enabled.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**TLS on port 587** is the most widely supported default across major providers including Gmail, Outlook, and Mailgun. Use SSL on port 465 only if your provider specifically requires it.
</Tip>

<Tip>
Always click **Send Test Email** after changing credentials or switching providers. A successful test confirms end-to-end delivery before any real customer emails are affected.
</Tip>
